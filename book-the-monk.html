The Monk

Matthew Lewis

✦ 1796  •  Gothic Horror  •  Public Domain ✦

Published in 1796 when Matthew Lewis was just nineteen years old, The Monk is one of the most extreme and transgressive works of the Gothic tradition. Set in Madrid, the novel follows Ambrosio, a celebrated and seemingly devout monk whose reputation for piety conceals a soul susceptible to the darkest temptations. When a mysterious stranger — later revealed to be the devil in disguise — enters his life, Ambrosio's fall is swift and catastrophic.

Lewis spared nothing in his portrayal of corruption, lust, murder, and diabolism. The novel caused scandal upon publication and was condemned as blasphemous and obscene, yet its influence on Gothic literature was enormous. The Monk sits at the darkest end of the Gothic spectrum, where religious hypocrisy meets supernatural horror and human weakness becomes absolute ruin.
