The Picture of Dorian Gray

Oscar Wilde

✦ 1890  •  Philosophical Fiction  •  Public Domain ✦

First published in Lippincott's Monthly Magazine in 1890, The Picture of Dorian Gray is Oscar Wilde's only novel and one of the most celebrated works of aesthetic and Gothic literature. The story follows the beautiful young Dorian Gray, who, entranced by the hedonistic philosophy of Lord Henry Wotton, wishes that his portrait would age in his place — a wish that is, terrifyingly, granted.

As the years pass, Dorian remains untouched by time while his portrait absorbs every sin and cruelty he commits. Wilde's novel is a meditation on vanity, moral corruption, and the price of a life lived purely in pursuit of pleasure. Beneath its elegant, witty surface lies one of literature's darkest explorations of conscience, beauty, and the soul.
