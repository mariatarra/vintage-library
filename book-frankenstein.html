Frankenstein

Mary Shelley

✦ 1818  •  Gothic Fiction  •  Public Domain ✦

First published anonymously in 1818, Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus is one of the earliest and most enduring works of science fiction and gothic literature. Written by Mary Shelley when she was just eighteen years old, the novel follows the ambitious scientist Victor Frankenstein, who, driven by an obsessive desire to conquer death, creates a living being from assembled corpse parts.

What follows is not a story of triumph, but of terror, abandonment, and tragic consequence. The creature, nameless and cast out, turns to darkness after facing nothing but rejection from the world that brought him into being. Shelley's novel raises questions that remain as urgent today as they were over two centuries ago: What are the limits of scientific ambition? What does it mean to be human? And what responsibility does a creator bear toward their creation?
